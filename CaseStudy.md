Governments, nonprofits, and businesses face complex challenges that require coordinated solutions. Through its US societal-impact initiatives, McKinsey & Company partners with public-sector institutions, nonprofits, and private organizations to address large-scale social and economic issues across the United States.

These initiatives focus on improving economic mobility, strengthening public systems, expanding workforce opportunities, and supporting communities through crises. McKinsey applies its analytical capabilities, research insights, and strategic advisory experience to help organizations design scalable solutions that deliver measurable impact.

One example of this work includes supporting large national initiatives aimed at improving workforce mobility. Programs such as the Rework America Alliance help workers transition from low-wage jobs into higher-paying roles by emphasizing skills-based hiring rather than traditional degree requirements.

Another area of impact involves strengthening government systems. McKinsey has collaborated with public agencies to improve services, enhance operational efficiency, and guide leadership transitions. Over the past several decades, the firm has helped guide more than 40 presidential, gubernatorial, and mayoral government transitions, supporting continuity and effectiveness in public leadership.

The firm also contributes to public-health and crisis-response initiatives. For example, in preparation for the launch of the US 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline, McKinsey worked with stakeholders to help expand mental-health support systems and improve response capacity. The program handled millions of calls, texts, and chats in its first year, significantly improving response times and increasing access to crisis support.

Across these initiatives, McKinsey’s approach combines data analysis, strategic design, and cross-sector collaboration to help organizations address societal challenges at scale.

The measurable outcomes from these initiatives demonstrate how consulting capabilities can extend beyond corporate transformation to drive social impact.

Key impact metrics include:

• Millions of calls, texts, and chats supported through the 988 crisis-response system in its first year
• More than 40 government leadership transitions supported through advisory initiatives
• Workforce initiatives supporting thousands of job seekers and coaches across dozens of US states
• Programs designed to expand economic mobility and improve access to career opportunities

This case highlights how consulting expertise can help governments, nonprofits, and communities address complex societal challenges through data-driven strategies and collaborative partnerships.
